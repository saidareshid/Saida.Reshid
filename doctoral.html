This page is dedicated to my doctoral studies in Cybersecurity Analytics at George Washington University. Here, you can find detailed information about my research, courses, and publications.

Courses

  • Challenges in Cybersecurity

    I honed my skills in vulnerability and threat management, focusing on analyzing threat data and managing security challenges specific to cloud technologies and specialized systems. I implemented robust security solutions, including Zero Trust architecture, and developed best practices for rapid and effective incident response. I engaged in proactive threat hunting and utilized advanced cybersecurity tools to master incident analysis. Additionally, I applied machine learning techniques to detect sophisticated cyber threats and integrated MITRE frameworks and other cybersecurity risk management strategies to safeguard digital assets effectively.

  • Tools for Cyber Analytics

    I developed a comprehensive set of skills for analyzing and securing IT infrastructures across various platforms. I gained proficiency in creating and implementing protection, detection, and forensic controls tailored to corporate, federal, and home environments. I learned to utilize a wide range of security tools, from data-centric security controls like Data Leak Prevention (DLP) and Content Disarm and Reconstruction (CDR) to advanced endpoint security analytics tools including Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) and Extended Detection and Response (XDR).


    The course also enhanced my understanding of secure cloud computing using Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM), Cloud Workload Protection Platforms (CWPP), and Cloud Access Security Brokers (CASB). I explored code-centric security analytics tools like Static and Dynamic Application Security Testing (SAST/DAST) and Software Composition Analysis (SCA) to ensure the security of software codes and supply-chain components.


    Moreover, I was trained in log-centric detection using tools like Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) and Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response (SOAR), along with user-centric tools for managing identities and behaviors through Identity and Access Management (IAM) and User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA). The program equipped me with skills in analyzing advanced persistent threats with simulation tools and frameworks, enhancing my ability to protect against and respond to sophisticated cyber-attacks effectively.

  • Cyber Forensics

    I acquired a great level of mastery related to applying forensic tools and techniques in collecting and analyzing data from endpoints and network traffic. I also learned how implementation of cybersecurity frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK and D3FEND affects the process of digital forensics. This course has equally enhanced my skills in the proper handling of digital evidence and conducting in-depth analyses of system memory and log files, which may be crucial for detecting intrusions into or breaches in security.


    This has further developed my forensic capabilities through intensive hands-on experience in using open-source threat intelligence tools, such as Metasploit and Wireshark, which deepen analysis skills in understanding and applying digital evidence. This has been effectively achieved through practical labs and real-world cases in the step-by-step methodology required in cyber investigations, especially in details regarding malware analysis, network forensics, and actionable intelligence extraction from compromised systems. This generalist training has equipped me with the essentials required in jobs that call for investigative precision and legal compliance within the field of cyber security.


    It has thus enabled me to develop and conduct the most complex cyber-forensic investigations within the appropriate legal frameworks requiring the highest levels of chain-of-evidence and maintaining integrity and confidentiality in the process of investigation. Integration of advanced forensic tools and methodologies conceptually developed my competencies in the preparation and presentation of detailed forensic reports and expert testimony for enforcement in the digital world.

  • Cybersecurity Architecture

    I deepened my proficiency in evaluating and implementing sophisticated cybersecurity architecture frameworks such as DevSecOps, cloud-native, and traditional network-centric models. I learned to discern between the functional and non-functional requirements that are relevant to building systems that are resilient and secure, emphasizing business objectives. The wide exposure to different architectural frameworks also provides insight into their security implications and the ability to propose optimal solutions that can be tailored toward the specific needs of an entity's digital transformation.


    The course structure emphasized crafting strategic cybersecurity decisions and the integration of frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK, D3FEND, and NIST's guidelines on cybersecurity architectures. I developed the ability to practice these standards and design proactive measures while blending the principles of the Secure by Design with Zero Trust models and Cyber Resilience tactics into organizational security postures. These experiences sharpened my skills in using architectural thought processes to see clearly and proactively mitigate possible security weaknesses in the IT infrastructure.


    Directly interfacing with the practical applications and hands-on exercises integrated within the course enables participants to learn how to implement and manage robust security architectures by using advanced tools and technologies. Some of these include mastering deep analytical skills and learning how to evaluate and fine-tune different tools to augment the security frameworks of enterprise IT environments. This practical knowledge prepares me to lead and innovate within cybersecurity architecture, ensuring security strategies are aligned with business and mission-critical operational needs.
