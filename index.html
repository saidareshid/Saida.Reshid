About Me

Saida Reshid, is a Doctoral Candidate in Cybersecurity Analytics at The George Washington University. She is also a Professional Data Scientist with a Master of Professional Studies in Data Science from University of Maryland Baltimore County and a Bachelor's in Mathematics, as a Meyerhoff Scholar.


Saida's professional pathway began during her undergraduate years with internships focusing on biological and molecular research at Johns Hopkins Medical Institute and the National Institutes of Health. Experiences from that period led to a way of incorporating mathematical skills in real biological applications and provided a platform for her to transition into more focused research-oriented roles.


After graduating, Saida entered the workforce at the University of Maryland and started working in Keller's lab on the role of serotonin in chronic pain using Python and data science. This consisted of developing algorithms for the analysis of physiological data from various animal models that help provide better insight into how differing serotonin level changes may give rise to chronic pain conditions. Her Python skills were invaluable as they facilitated the deciphering of complex datasets and enabled major research findings.


She then transitioned to a Data Scientist position at Oncospace applying her scientific depth from previous research on advanced data science methods to improve radiation dose predictions for improved planning and outcomes in radiation therapy for targeted cancer treatment. This was designing and developing supervised and unsupervised machine learning models related to clustering and decision trees, in regard to answering the question of the therapeutic outcome.


During Saida's data science master's program, she decided to choose a cybersecurity pathway which led to the development of her strong interest in cybersecurity, leading to her pursuit in a Graduate Certificate in Cybersecurity Operations. As she continued to explore the intersection of data science and cybersecurity, it became evident that pursuing a doctorate in Cybersecurity Analytics was the natural progression of her career. This decision was driven by her extensive research background and a keen interest in leveraging cybersecurity to safeguard digital infrastructures. Saida's future research will be on integrating cybersecurity and artificial intelligence to pioneer innovative approaches in digital security using her expertise in data science.

My Projects



Snort Challenge: The Basics

Snort Challenge: The Basics

In the "Snort Challenge — The Basics" project on TryHackme, Saida honed her network security skills by crafting and implementing a series of Intrusion Detection System (IDS) rules using Snort. This project encompassed setting up Snort, analyzing network traffic, and creating rules for HTTP and FTP traffic, PNG and GIF file detection, and complex threats like MS17-010 and Log4j exploits. This not only solidified her proficiency in using Snort but also enhanced her ability to apply theoretical knowledge practically, making it a standout addition to her professional portfolio.



Wireshark: Capturing and Analyzing Network Pack

Wireshark: Capturing and Analyzing Network Pack

Saida used Wireshark on Ubuntu to capture and analyze network packets, enhancing security protocols and analyzing HTTPS traffic with advanced filters. She managed network interfaces for secure data capture and set up TLS handshake filters to identify specific IP communications. Her expertise in using network protocol analysis tools significantly boosted her capabilities in network monitoring and cybersecurity defense, making her a key asset in any security team.



Windows Security: Project on Defender and Firewall

Windows Security: A Comprehesnive Project on Defender and Firewall

Saida took a hands-on project regarding the security features of Microsoft Windows, particularly on Windows Defender and Firewall. The project comprised several labs where she learned to navigate seamlessly in the Windows Security area, with a particular focus on critical functions of real-time detection of threats, facilities for system scanning, and reliable firewall settings. Saida conducted a detailed review, checked the updated definition of the virus, ran and analyzed the quick/custom scans of the antivirus, and then set up the firewall rules with more depth in setting measures for the respective network domains. She documented each step followed in detail to be able to reproduce such results and as a good practice in cybersecurity. This project has further honed her technical skill in working with key security tools within Windows, and increased her capacity to carry out very accurate and effective security protocols in any given digital infrastructure.
